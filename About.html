Learn About Organ Donation...

How Does It Works?



Who Can Donate?

Anyone can become an organ donor, regardless of age or ethnicity. Medical suitability is determined at the time of donation.

  • Living donors: Donate a kidney or part of their liver.
  • Deceased donors: Donate viable organs after passing away.
  • People of all ages can be donors. The suitability of organs depends on medical assessment, not age.

Why is Organ Donation Important?

  • Life-Saving: One donor can save up to eight lives through organ donation and enhance many more through tissue donation.
  • High Demand: Thousands of people worldwide are on organ transplant waiting lists.

    • How to Become a Donor?

  • Sign Up.
  • Register with your country’s organ donation registry.
  • In some countries, you can indicate your sdecision on your driver’s license or identification card.
  • Inform Your Family.
  • Let your loved ones know your decision, as they may need to consent to the donation.
  • Health and Medical History.
    • Donors go through a medical assessment to ensure their organs are healthy and suitable for transplantation.

    What Can I Donate?

    Get Facts Now

    • Over 100,000 people are currently waiting for organ transplants worldwide.
    • A single donor can save up to 8 lives and improve quality of life for over 75 others.
    • Organs such as heart, kidneys, liver, and lungs can be donated.
    • Both living and deceased individuals can donate organs.
    • Tissue donations, like corneas and skin, can improve the lives of over 75 people.
    • Globally, thousands of people die each year waiting for organ transplants.
    • In the U.S., over 100,000 people are currently on the transplant waiting list
    • Donors and their families are not responsible for any costs related to the organ donation process.
    • The identity of both the donor and the recipient is kept confidential in most case

    How Organ Donors Change Lives...

    Organ donors make a tremendous impact by:

    • Saving lives: Offering survival to patients with failing organs.
    • Improving quality of life: Allowing recipients to regain active lifestyles.
    • Offering hope: Providing comfort to donor families through their loved one's legacy.
